The Sunshine Building

Stonecloud Brewering Company found its home within the historic Sunshine Laundry Cleaners building at NW 1st and Classen on the west side of downtown OKC. The building, originally constructed in 1929, served as a valuable asset to the community until it fell into neglect in 1983 after....

Before the renovations began, the Sunshine Building had been vacant for an estimated 30 years and had been without a roof for more than a decade. Graffiti covered the walls and most of the windows were broken. Pigeons living in the building numbered in the hundreds and a rusted out boat was hanging from the trusses. A tree with an 8 inch trunk was growing out of the concrete where our fermenters now stand.

It took a lot of imagination to envision what the building could become, but we were committed to the project and the idea of breathing new life into the old brick structure. We placed the building on the National Historic Registry and got right to work.

Over the course of a year and a half we painstakingly restored the iconic building, and each day a semblance of the original building emerged. It was clear that we had not made fault in our undertaking. The walls and windows, which once were cold and uninviting, now resonate with warmth and luster. The space was destined for new purpose and will greet new visitors with a vibrance... The final touch of course was to restore the iconic neon sign with its original brilliance and animations.

We have been enormously fortunate to partner with Pivot Projects in bringing the building back to life, and we are excited to share this beautiful space with everyone who comes to visit.

To learn more about the renovation and experience the transformation please visit Stonecloud Brewing Company on Facebook and Instagram. You won't believe it's the same building!
